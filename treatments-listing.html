Need for IVF Procedure

IVF is a common infertility treatment procedure for a large variety of male and female infertility disorders including but not limited to:

Female:
  1. Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  2. Ovulation disorders
  3. Endometriosis
  4. Uterine Fibroids
Male:
  1. Low sperm count
  2. Poor sperm quality
  3. Sperm is unable to fertilize the egg on its own
  4. When there are no sperms in the ejaculate but sperms can be taken from the testes

IVF Process

  1. Initial Assessment – Ultrasound scan for the female partner and semen analysis for the male partner are conducted to ensure that IVF is the right treatment for the couple to be able to get pregnant.
  2. Hormone Stimulation – In this phase, the female partner is advised to take fertility medicines continuously for a few months to ensure that her ovaries produce a large number of eggs that are mature and ready to be fertilized.
  3. Egg Retrieval – This is a minor surgical procedure usually performed with anaesthesia where the fluid contained in the follicles is extracted and handed over to the embryologist who separates the eggs from it and places them in the incubator.
  4. Sperm Preparation – The sperm sample from the male partner is obtained and processed to extract the best appearing sperms that will be used to fertilize the eggs in the lab.
  5. Fertilization – The eggs and the sperms are mixed and stored in a special container where they have high chances of finding each other as they naturally would within the body.
  6. Embryo Transfer and Implantation – In 3 to 5 days of egg retrieval, 1 or more embryos are obtained, the healthiest one of them all is loaded into a plastic tube, passed through the cervix, and transferred to the uterus. Pregnancy occurs only when the embryo attaches itself to the uterus lining.
